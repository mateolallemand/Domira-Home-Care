What Is Personal Care?

Personal care services provide hands-on assistance with the Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) that may become difficult due to aging, illness, or disability. Our trained caregivers help with these essential tasks while preserving your dignity and independence.

Services Include:

  • Bathing and showering assistance
  • Grooming and personal hygiene
  • Dressing assistance
  • Toileting and incontinence care
  • Mobility and transfer assistance
  • Positioning and comfort support
  • Medication reminders
  • Feeding assistance

Who Benefits from Personal Care?

  • Seniors who need daily living assistance
  • Individuals recovering from surgery or illness
  • People with chronic conditions or disabilities
  • Those who want to remain independent at home

Alzheimer's & Dementia Care

Compassionate, specialized support for individuals living with cognitive decline.

What Is Alzheimer's & Dementia Care?

Caring for a loved one with Alzheimer's disease or another form of dementia requires patience, consistency, and specialized knowledge. Domira Home Care provides trained caregivers who understand the unique challenges of cognitive decline and are equipped to support your loved one with dignity, safety, and compassion in the comfort of their own home.

Our approach emphasizes routine, familiarity, and coordination. We work closely with families and, when appropriate, with physicians, home health providers, hospice, and other members of the care team to ensure a seamless, well-coordinated experience.

Services Include:

  • Supervision and safety monitoring throughout the day
  • Assistance with daily routines — bathing, dressing, grooming, meals, and mobility
  • Medication reminders and appointment accompaniment
  • Cognitive engagement activities and gentle redirection
  • Consistent caregiver matching to build trust and familiarity
  • Wandering prevention and fall risk management
  • Coordination with home health, hospice, and physicians when appropriate
  • Emotional support and companionship to reduce isolation

Signs Your Loved One May Benefit from Dementia Care:

  • Forgetting familiar people, places, or daily tasks
  • Confusion about time, location, or surroundings
  • Difficulty managing medications or personal hygiene
  • Wandering, agitation, or sundowning behavior
  • Repeating questions or conversations frequently
  • Struggling to complete routine activities they once did easily
  • Family caregivers feeling overwhelmed or burned out

7 Signs Your Parent May Need Help at Home

Recognizing when it's time to consider in-home care.

It can be hard to know when a parent or loved one needs extra support. Here are some common signs that in-home care could help:

1. Difficulty with Personal Hygiene

Skipping showers, wearing the same clothes, unkempt appearance, or noticeable body odor.

2. Changes in Eating Habits

Weight loss, expired food in the fridge, missed meals, or difficulty cooking.

3. Increased Forgetfulness

Missing medications, forgetting appointments, leaving the stove on, or repeating conversations.

4. Declining Mobility

Unsteadiness, holding onto furniture, falls or near-falls, difficulty getting up from chairs.

5. Social Withdrawal

Avoiding activities they used to enjoy, not answering the phone, losing interest in hobbies.

6. Messy or Unsafe Home

Piled up mail, dirty dishes, cluttered walkways, or missed household maintenance.

7. Caregiver Burnout

If you're the primary caregiver and feeling exhausted, overwhelmed, or unable to keep up.

Home Care vs Home Health Care

What's the Difference?

Home Care (Non-Medical)

  • Personal care assistance (bathing, dressing)
  • Companion care and supervision
  • Meal preparation and housekeeping
  • Transportation and errands
  • Medication reminders
  • No doctor's order required
  • Paid privately or through LTC insurance

Home Health Care (Medical)

  • Skilled nursing care
  • Physical, occupational, speech therapy
  • Wound care and injections
  • Medical monitoring
  • Requires a doctor's order
  • Typically covered by Medicare
  • Usually short-term / episodic

Domira Home Care provides non-medical personal assistant services.

We focus on helping individuals live independently at home through daily living support — not clinical or medical care.

Many families use both services together. We're happy to coordinate with your home health provider.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our services and how we can help.

How quickly can services start?
In most cases, we can begin services within 24-48 hours of completing a care assessment. For urgent situations, we do our best to accommodate same-day or next-day starts.
Is home care the same as medical care?
Home care provides non-medical support that helps individuals remain safe, comfortable, and independent at home. Guided by clinical leadership, the Domira care professionals assist with daily activities such as bathing, dressing, meals, companionship, and household support while maintaining attentive observation and communication with families. Our services complement medical providers such as home health, hospice, or therapy.
Can care be scheduled just a few hours per week?
Absolutely. We offer flexible scheduling from just a few hours a week to 24/7 live-in care. We'll work with you to build a schedule that fits your needs and budget.
Do caregivers stay overnight?
Yes, we offer overnight and extended-hour care for clients who need support during nighttime hours. This includes supervision, toileting assistance, and safety monitoring.
Does long-term care insurance cover home care?
Many long-term care insurance policies cover non-medical home care services. We can help you understand your policy and navigate the claims process. Contact us for a free benefits review.
How are caregivers screened?
All Domira caregivers undergo thorough background checks, reference verification, and skills assessment. We carefully match caregivers to clients based on personality, experience, and care needs.
What if I don't get along with my caregiver?
We understand that fit matters. If a caregiver isn't the right match, we'll promptly reassign a new caregiver at no additional cost. Your comfort and trust are our priority.
What areas do you serve?
We currently serve Frisco, Plano, McKinney, Prosper, Little Elm, Aubrey, The Colony, North Dallas, and surrounding North Texas communities. Contact us to confirm service in your area.
