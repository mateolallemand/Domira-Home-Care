How Much Does Home Care Cost in North Texas?

Domira Home Care · March 2026 · North Texas

One of the first questions families ask when exploring home care is about cost. Here's a transparent breakdown of what to expect in North Texas.

Factors That Affect Cost

Level of care needed. Personal care (bathing, dressing, mobility assistance) requires more training and hands-on time than companion care or light housekeeping. The more involved the care, the higher the hourly rate.

Hours per week. Most agencies have a minimum number of hours per visit, typically 3-4 hours. Families who need more hours per week may be able to negotiate a lower hourly rate. Full-time or live-in care has different pricing structures.

Time of day. Overnight and weekend care may have different rates than standard daytime hours during the week.

Duration of care. Short-term recovery support after surgery vs. long-term ongoing care may affect overall pricing and scheduling flexibility.

Average Rates in North Texas

In the North Texas area, most private-pay home care services fall in the $30 to $40 per hour range depending on the type and level of care needed. While statewide averages are often quoted lower, the higher cost of living in the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex pushes caregiver rates above state averages. Families in Frisco, Plano, McKinney, and surrounding cities should plan accordingly.

Payment Options

Private pay is the most common method for non-medical home care. Many families split costs among siblings or combine savings with other funding sources.

Long-term care insurance often covers in-home care services. We can help you understand your policy and navigate the claims process.

Veterans benefits through the VA Aid & Attendance program can help qualifying veterans and spouses cover home care costs.

Home Care vs Other Options

Assisted living in North Texas averages $4,000-$6,000 per month. Nursing homes can run $7,000-$10,000 or more. At 20 hours per week, in-home care costs roughly $2,400-$3,200 per month, which is often less than facility-based care while allowing your loved one to stay in their own home.
