How Much Does Home Care Cost in Texas?

Domira Home Care · March 2026 · North Texas

When a parent or loved one starts needing help at home, one of the first questions families ask is: how much is this going to cost? The answer depends on several factors, but here's what you can realistically expect in the North Texas area.

Average Home Care Costs in Texas

Non-medical home care in Texas typically ranges from $20 to $30 per hour depending on the level of care needed, the specific services required, and the agency you work with. This covers personal care, companion care, homemaking, and similar non-medical assistance.

For context, Texas is below the national average for home care costs. States like California and New York often run $30-$40+ per hour. That makes Texas one of the more affordable states for quality in-home care.

What Affects the Price

Level of care needed. Basic companion care (conversation, light housekeeping, meal prep) costs less than personal care (bathing, dressing, mobility assistance, toileting). The more hands-on the care, the higher the hourly rate.

Hours per week. Most agencies have a minimum number of hours per visit (typically 3-4 hours). Families who need more hours per week may be able to negotiate a lower hourly rate. Full-time or live-in care has different pricing structures.

Schedule. Overnight shifts, weekends, and holidays may cost more. Standard daytime hours during the week are typically the most affordable.

Caregiver qualifications. Agencies that invest in thorough background checks, training, and ongoing supervision charge more than independent caregivers found online, but you're paying for accountability, reliability, and peace of mind.

Home Care vs Other Options

Assisted living facilities in Texas average $3,500-$5,000 per month. Nursing homes run $5,000-$8,000+ per month. If your loved one only needs help a few hours a day, in-home care at $20-30/hour is significantly more affordable and lets them stay in the comfort of their own home.

Even at 20 hours per week, in-home care runs roughly $1,600-$2,400/month. That's less than most assisted living facilities and far less than a nursing home.

How to Pay for Home Care

Long-term care insurance often covers in-home care. Check the policy for details on daily maximums and qualifying conditions.

Veterans benefits through the VA Aid & Attendance program can help cover home care costs for qualifying veterans and their spouses.

Medicaid in Texas covers some in-home care services through the STAR+PLUS program for eligible individuals.

Private pay is the most common method. Many families split costs among siblings or use a combination of savings, insurance, and benefits.
