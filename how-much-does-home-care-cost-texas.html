How Much Does Home Care Cost in Texas?

Domira Home Care · March 2026 · North Texas

Understanding the cost of home care is often one of the first and most important questions families ask. The answer depends on location, level of care, and scheduling needs.

While statewide averages in Texas are often quoted in the mid-$20s per hour, those numbers don't always reflect what families actually experience in North Texas. For families in areas like Frisco, McKinney, Plano, and Prosper, most private-pay home care services typically fall in the $30 to $40 per hour range, depending on the type and level of care needed.

At Domira Home Care, we believe in transparent, straightforward pricing without surprises so families can plan with confidence.

What Affects the Cost of Home Care?

The cost of in-home care isn't one-size-fits-all. Several factors influence pricing:

Level of care. Companion care (light support and supervision) is generally less expensive than hands-on personal care, which includes assistance with bathing, mobility, and hygiene. Specialized care for individuals with Alzheimer's or dementia may require additional attention and structure.

Number of hours. Hourly rates may vary depending on how many hours of care are scheduled each week. More consistent schedules often allow for more efficient care planning.

Time of day. Overnight care, weekends, and 24-hour care may be priced differently than standard daytime hours.

Specialized needs. Care for individuals with complex mobility needs, cognitive decline, or post-surgical recovery may require caregivers with specific experience and training.

What Does Home Care Include?

Home care services typically include a combination of personal care (bathing, dressing, hygiene), companion care and social support, meal preparation, light housekeeping, medication reminders, mobility and safety assistance, and transportation and errands. Each care plan is customized to fit the individual's needs and preferences.

How to Estimate Your Monthly Cost

A simple way to estimate what home care might cost for your family:

  • 4 hours/day, 5 days/week at $32/hour = approximately $2,560/month
  • 4 hours/day, 7 days/week at $32/hour = approximately $3,580/month
  • 8 hours/day, 5 days/week at $32/hour = approximately $5,120/month

Actual costs vary based on your specific situation, but this provides a general idea of what to expect in the North Texas area.

Is Home Care Covered by Insurance?

In most cases, non-medical home care is considered private-pay, meaning it is not covered by Medicare. However, some families may be able to use long-term care insurance, which often covers in-home care services. Veterans benefits may also help in certain cases. We're happy to help families understand their options during a free consultation.

Why Home Care Is Often More Affordable Than Facilities

Many families are surprised to learn that home care can be a cost-effective alternative to assisted living or skilled nursing facilities. Assisted living in North Texas averages $4,000 to $6,000 per month. Nursing homes can run $7,000 to $10,000 or more.

With home care, you only pay for the hours you need, allowing for greater flexibility and control over costs. It also allows your loved one to remain in a familiar environment, which can significantly improve comfort, independence, and overall wellbeing.
