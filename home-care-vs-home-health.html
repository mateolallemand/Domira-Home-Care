Home Care vs Home Health Care: What's the Difference?

Domira Home Care · March 2026 · North Texas

These two terms sound similar but they're very different services. Understanding the distinction helps you get the right support for your family.

Home Care (Non-Medical)

Home care provides non-medical support that helps individuals remain safe, comfortable, and independent at home. This includes personal care (bathing, dressing, grooming), companion care (conversation, supervision, outings), homemaking (meals, cleaning, laundry), medication reminders, and transportation. No doctor's order is required, and it's typically paid privately or through long-term care insurance.

Home Health Care (Medical)

Home health care is medical care provided in the home by licensed professionals. This includes skilled nursing, physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy, wound care, and injections. It requires a doctor's order and is typically covered by Medicare. Home health is usually short-term and episodic, focused on recovery or managing a specific medical condition.

Key Differences

Who provides it: Home care is provided by trained caregivers. Home health is provided by nurses, therapists, and other licensed medical professionals.

What it covers: Home care covers daily living support. Home health covers clinical and medical treatments.

How it's paid for: Home care is private pay or long-term care insurance. Home health is typically covered by Medicare.

How long it lasts: Home care can be ongoing for months or years. Home health is usually short-term, tied to a specific medical need.

Who decides: You can arrange home care on your own. Home health requires a physician's order.

Can You Use Both?

Yes, and many families do. A common scenario: your loved one receives home health visits from a nurse twice a week for wound care after surgery, while a home care aide comes daily to help with bathing, meals, and companionship. The two services complement each other.
