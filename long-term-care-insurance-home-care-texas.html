Does Long-Term Care Insurance Cover Home Care in Texas?

Domira Home Care · June 2026 · North Texas

For families paying privately for home care, long-term care insurance is one of the most useful and most misunderstood resources available. If your parent bought a policy years ago, it may cover a meaningful share of non-medical home care. Here is how it generally works in Texas and what you need to actually use it.

Does It Cover Non-Medical Home Care?

In most cases, yes. The majority of long-term care policies are designed to pay for help with daily living, which is exactly what non-medical home care provides: bathing, dressing, mobility, meals, supervision, and companionship. This is different from health insurance or Medicare, which generally do not pay for ongoing non-medical care at home. Older or very basic policies sometimes limit coverage to facility care only, so the wording of the specific policy matters.

How Policies Pay

Most policies share a few common features that determine what you actually receive:

  • Daily or monthly benefit. The maximum the policy pays toward care over a given period.
  • Elimination period. A waiting window, often 30 to 90 days, that you pay out of pocket before benefits begin.
  • Benefit period. How long benefits last, whether a set number of years or a total pool of money.
  • Inflation protection. Whether the benefit grows over time, which matters for policies bought long ago.

What Triggers Coverage

Benefits usually begin once the insured person needs help with a set number of activities of daily living, commonly two or more, such as bathing, dressing, eating, transferring, toileting, or continence. Cognitive decline that requires supervision, such as dementia, also typically qualifies. An assessment documents the need, and a plan of care defines the support.

What You Need to File a Claim

Insurers generally want a current care assessment, a written plan of care, and ongoing records of the services delivered. A home care provider that documents care clearly makes this far easier. Keeping organized records of visits and the support provided is what keeps a claim moving.

Private Pay and Long-Term Care Insurance Together

Many families use both. They pay privately during the elimination period and for any gap between the policy benefit and the actual cost of care, then let the policy cover the rest. Understanding the daily benefit against local home care rates helps you plan the real out-of-pocket number.
