How to Find a Reliable Caregiver in North Texas

Domira Home Care · March 2026 · North Texas

Finding the right caregiver is one of the most important decisions you'll make for your family. You're trusting someone to be in your loved one's home, sometimes when no one else is around. Here's how to find someone you can trust in the North Texas area.

Agency vs Independent Caregiver

Home care agencies handle the hiring, background checks, training, scheduling, and supervision. If a caregiver calls in sick, the agency sends a replacement. You're paying more per hour, but you're paying for reliability, accountability, and peace of mind.

Independent caregivers found through online platforms or word of mouth are typically cheaper. But you're responsible for verifying their background, handling taxes (they're technically your employee), managing scheduling, and finding a backup if they can't show up. If something goes wrong, there's no agency to step in.

For most families, especially those new to home care, an agency is the safer choice.

What to Look For in an Agency

Background checks. Every caregiver should undergo a criminal background check, reference verification, and skills assessment. Ask the agency what their screening process looks like.

Training. Caregivers should be trained in the specific services they're providing. If your loved one needs help with transfers or mobility, the caregiver needs to know proper techniques.

Supervision. Good agencies don't just send a caregiver and disappear. They should have a care coordinator who checks in regularly, adjusts the care plan as needs change, and is available when you have concerns.

Insurance and bonding. The agency should carry liability insurance and be bonded. This protects you if something goes wrong.

Flexibility. Your needs will change. The agency should be able to increase or decrease hours, adjust schedules, and add services without making you feel locked into a rigid contract.

Questions to Ask During a Consultation

  • How do you screen and train your caregivers?
  • What happens if our regular caregiver is sick or unavailable?
  • How do you match caregivers with clients?
  • Is there a minimum number of hours per visit?
  • What's included in your hourly rate?
  • Can we meet the caregiver before they start?
  • How do you handle concerns or complaints?

Red Flags to Watch For

No background checks. If an agency can't clearly explain their screening process, walk away.

Long-term contracts. Reputable agencies don't lock you into year-long commitments. You should be able to adjust or cancel with reasonable notice.

No in-person assessment. Any good agency will want to meet your loved one and assess their needs before starting care. If they're willing to send someone over without an assessment, that's a red flag.

Vague pricing. You should get a clear, written breakdown of costs before care begins. No hidden fees, no surprises.
